    what we do

    • motivated in part by the financial difficulties facing the International Union for Conservation of Nature (IUCN), and felt that a new fundraising initiative might help IUCN and other conservation groups carry out their mission.
    • "Turtle Foundation" grants $38,000 to the Smithsonian Institution to study the tiger population of the Chitwan Sanctuary in Nepal, allowing scientists to successfully use radio tracking devices for the first time in 1973.
    • "Turtle Foundation" starts to focus not only on species-related conservation projects, but also on protecting habitat by establishing national parks and nature reserves.
